Masashi kishimoto

Creator of Naruto

Time line of Masashi Kishimoro

  • 1976 - Born in Nagi, Japan.
  • 1995 - Kishimoto's first successful manga pilot was Karakuri.
  • 1996 - Honorable mention in Shueisha's monthly "Hop Step Award" as promising rookie manga artist.
  • 1997 - He wrote a one-shot version of Naruto (NARUTO－ナルト－) which was published in Akamaru Jump Summer.
  • 1998 - Failure of Karakuri, worked in various mangas like baseball manga, Yakyūō, mafia manga, Mario and fantasy one-shot, Magic Mushroom.
  • 1999 - Start working on Naruto serialisation, it quickly become hit.
  • 2006 - 10% manga sales in USA, getting world's attendtion, it become the first manga to ever win a Quill Award when it claimed the award for "Best Graphic Novel" in 2006.
  • 2010 - Kishimoto produced a one-shot baseball manga, Bench (ベンチ, Benchi), as part of Jump's "Top of the Super Legend" project, a series of six one-shot manga by famed Weekly Shōnen Jump artists.
  • 2012 - Kishimoto publish a one-shot version of his long-postponed mafia manga, Mario, in Jump Square,[43] based on the rough, 160-page manuscript he began working on before Naruto became serialized.
  • 2013 - Several of Kishimoto's one-shots saw their English-language debut in issues of the Weekly Shonen Jump digital magazine, including Mario, Bench, and the original Naruto pilot.
  • 2014 - Naruto serialisation ended after more than 15 years, with total of 700 chaptersin 72 volumes.
  • 2015 - Kishimoto, released spinoff The miniseries, Naruto: The Seventh Hokage and the Scarlet Spring, ran from April to July 2015, leading up to the premiere of Boruto: Naruto the Movie on August 7, 2015, which he supervised and co-wrote with Ukyo Kodachi.
  • 2017 - At Jump Festa 2018 it was confirmed that Kishimoto was developing a new science fiction adventure series tentatively scheduled to debut in 2018
  • 2019 - At Jump Festa 2019 the series was formally announced as Samurai 8: The Tale of Hachimaru.
  • 2020 - It was announced that after 51 chapters and 13 volumes Kodachi would step down as writer of the Boruto: Naruto Next Generations manga, with Kishimoto assuming full writing duties.
